Alerts ▼

Show important messages with different alert styles.

Success! Your action was successful.
Info: This is an informational message. ×
Warning! Please be cautious. ×
Error! Something went wrong. ×

Use this HTML code:

    <div class="alert alert-success">
        <strong>Success!</strong> Your action was successful.
        <span class="alert" ></span>
    </div>
    <div class="alert alert-info">
        <strong>Info:</strong> This is an informational message.
        <span class="alert-close" onclick="closeAlert(this)">×</span>
    </div>
    <div class="alert alert-warning">
        <strong>Warning!</strong> Please be cautious.
        <span class="alert-close" onclick="closeAlert(this)">×</span>
    </div>
    <div class="alert alert-danger">
        <strong>Error!</strong> Something went wrong.
        <span class="alert-close" onclick="closeAlert(this)">×</span>
    </div>

Container ▼

The .container class provides a fixed-width layout, centered on the page.

Example:

Column 1
Column 2
Column 3

Use this HTML code:

    <div class="container">
        <div class="row">
            <div class="col-4" style="background: lightblue;">Column 1</div>
            <div class="col-4" style="background: lightcoral;">Column 2</div>
            <div class="col-3" style="background: lightblue;">Column 3</div>
        </div>
    </div>

Container-Fluid ▼

The .container-fluid class provides a full-width layout, spanning the entire viewport.

Example:

Column 1
Column 2

Use this HTML code:

    <div class="container-fluid">
        <div class="row">
            <div class="col-6" style="background: lightblue;">Column 1</div>
            <div class="col-6" style="background: lightcoral;">Column 2</div>
        </div>
    </div>

Image Responsive ▼

The .img-responsive class makes the image responsive, meaning it will adjust its size based on the viewport width.

Example:

Use this HTML code:

    <img src="https://placehold.co/600x400" alt="LED" class="img-responsive">

Visibility Test ▼

Control visibility of elements on different screen sizes and print media.

Visible on extra small screens (xs)
Visible on small screens (sm) and larger
Visible on medium screens (md) and larger
Visible on large screens (lg) and larger
Visible on extra-large screens (xl) and larger
Hidden on extra small screens (xs)
Hidden on small screens (sm) and larger
Hidden on medium screens (md) and larger
Hidden on large screens (lg) and larger
Hidden on extra-large screens (xl) and larger
Visible only when printed
Hidden when printed

Use this HTML code:

<div class="box visible-xs">Visible on extra small screens (xs)</div>
<div class="box visible-sm">Visible on small screens (sm) and larger</div>
<div class="box visible-md">Visible on medium screens (md) and larger</div>
<div class="box visible-lg">Visible on large screens (lg) and larger</div>
<div class="box visible-xl">Visible on extra-large screens (xl) and larger</div>

<div class="box hidden-xs">Hidden on extra small screens (xs)</div>
<div class="box hidden-sm">Hidden on small screens (sm) and larger</div>
<div class="box hidden-md">Hidden on medium screens (md) and larger</div>
<div class="box hidden-lg">Hidden on large screens (lg) and larger</div>
<div class="box hidden-xl">Hidden on extra-large screens (xl) and larger</div>

<div class="box visible-print">Visible only when printed</div>
<div class="box hidden-print">Hidden when printed</div>
